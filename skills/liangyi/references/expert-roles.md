# Expert Role Pairs Reference

## By Product Category

| Product Type | Role A (Claude) | Role B (Gemini) | Tension Point |
|-------------|----------------|-----------------|---------------|
| Payments / Finance | Security Architect | Growth Hacker | Safety vs speed-to-market |
| Social / Content | UX Designer | Data Monetization Lead | User delight vs revenue extraction |
| Tools / Productivity | Minimalist PM | Full-Stack Architect | Simplicity vs feature completeness |
| AI / Technical | AI Researcher | Commercialization Director | Technical purity vs business viability |
| Education | Educational Psychologist | Gamification Designer | Depth of learning vs engagement |
| Healthcare | Clinical Safety Officer | Patient Experience Designer | Regulatory compliance vs usability |
| E-commerce | Supply Chain Analyst | Conversion Optimizer | Operations reliability vs sales push |

## Universal High-Tension Pairs

| Role A | Role B | Core Conflict |
|--------|--------|---------------|
| VC Investor | Senior Engineer | ROI & scale vs feasibility & maintenance cost |
| Industry Veteran | Cross-Domain Newcomer | Barriers & experience vs disruption & first principles |
| Target User Advocate | Competitive Analyst | Pain point depth vs market differentiation |
| CTO | CFO | Technical excellence vs cost control |
| Designer | Data Scientist | Intuition & craft vs measurement & optimization |

## Selection Rules

1. **The tension test:** If both roles would give the same top-3 recommendations, the pair has insufficient tension. Pick another.
2. **The relevance test:** Both roles must have legitimate authority over the product domain. Don't pair a "chef" with an "astronaut" for a fintech product just for tension.
3. **The coverage test:** Between the two roles, most critical dimensions (technical, market, user, financial) should be covered.
